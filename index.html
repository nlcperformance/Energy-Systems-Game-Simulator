The Energy Continuum

All movement requires energy (ATP). The human body utilizes three distinct biological engines to turn chemical fuel into physical motion, dependent entirely on intensity and duration.

ATP-PC System

Alactic / Anaerobic

Fuel: Phosphocreatine (PCr)

Duration: 0 - 10 seconds

Yield: 1 ATP per PCr (Low)

By-product: None

PROS

Instant energy. Extremely powerful. No fatiguing by-products.

CONS

Depletes rapidly. Takes 2-3 minutes to fully recover (EPOC).

Anaerobic Glycolysis

Lactate System

Fuel: Glycogen (Carbs)

Duration: 10s - 3 minutes

Yield: 2 ATP per Glucose

By-product: Lactic Acid (H+ ions)

PROS

Fast energy production without needing oxygen. Good for surges.

CONS (OBLA)

Lactic acid drops blood pH, inhibiting the PFK enzyme. Causes severe muscle fatigue.

Aerobic System

Mitochondrial

Fuel: Glycogen & Fats

Duration: 3 minutes - Hours

Yield: 38 ATP per Glucose

By-product: CO2 & Water

PROS

Massive ATP yield. Clears lactic acid. Harmless by-products.

CONS

Slow to activate. Requires constant oxygen delivery. Lower peak power.
